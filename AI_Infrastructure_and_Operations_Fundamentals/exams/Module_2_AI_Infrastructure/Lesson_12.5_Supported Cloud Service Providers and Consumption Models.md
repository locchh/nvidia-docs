### Cloud Consumption Models

**6. What is the traditional on-premises model of IT infrastructure?**

A. A model that relies on cloud-based solutions exclusively  
B. A setup where organizations manage their own servers, storage, and networking equipment on-site  
C. A model that is solely based on public cloud services  
D. A system that requires no maintenance  

**Answer:** B. A setup where organizations manage their own servers, storage, and networking equipment on-site

---

**7. How does the responsibility for managing technology stacks differ between on-premises and cloud environments?**

A. Organizations have the same responsibilities in both environments  
B. In cloud environments, the CSP manages more aspects of the technology stack, reducing the burden on the organization  
C. Organizations are less responsible for security in cloud environments  
D. Cloud environments require more hardware management  

**Answer:** B. In cloud environments, the CSP manages more aspects of the technology stack, reducing the burden on the organization

---

**8. What are the key characteristics of Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)?**

A. Users have full control over the hardware and software environment, but the CSP manages the physical infrastructure  
B. Users have no control over the infrastructure  
C. IaaS includes fully managed applications  
D. IaaS is only for large enterprises  

**Answer:** A. Users have full control over the hardware and software environment, but the CSP manages the physical infrastructure

---

**9. How does Platform as a Service (PaaS) differ from IaaS?**

A. PaaS offers more hardware control than IaaS  
B. PaaS provides a platform for developers to build applications without managing the underlying infrastructure, whereas IaaS offers raw infrastructure resources  
C. IaaS is only for development environments  
D. There is no difference between PaaS and IaaS  

**Answer:** B. PaaS provides a platform for developers to build applications without managing the underlying infrastructure, whereas IaaS offers raw infrastructure resources

---

**10. What does Software as a Service (SaaS) entail, and what responsibilities does it shift to the CSP?**

A. SaaS provides applications over the internet and shifts the responsibility for infrastructure and application maintenance to the CSP  
B. SaaS requires organizations to manage their own applications  
C. SaaS is only available on-premises  
D. SaaS is not a viable business model  

**Answer:** A. SaaS provides applications over the internet and shifts the responsibility for infrastructure and application maintenance to the CSP
